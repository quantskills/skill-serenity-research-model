# Serenity Model Axes

Use these axes when interpreting extracted signals.

## Research Starting Point

Serenity-style research usually starts from a large AI infrastructure demand wave, then moves upstream to less obvious bottlenecks. The key question is not "which famous company wins?" but "which constrained supplier benefits if many famous companies must spend?"

## Bottleneck Logic

Look for claims that a company controls or benefits from:

- scarce physical inputs, such as InP, silicon photonics materials, HBM, fiber, power equipment, cooling, or packaging capacity
- hard-to-replace equipment, test, inspection, or manufacturing steps
- long qualification cycles or customer lock-in
- under-covered suppliers below the obvious mega-cap layer
- capacity expansion that may create nonlinear operating leverage

## Evidence Preference

Classify evidence into:

- customer or hyperscaler capex link
- supplier/customer relationship
- technical constraint
- capacity and utilization
- financial inflection
- patent or scientific signal
- management commentary
- regulatory or policy catalyst
- peer move or sympathy basket
- price/volume confirmation

## Conviction Signals

Higher conviction is implied by:

- repeated mentions across days or threads
- explicit "highest conviction" language
- long-form thesis with specific mechanisms
- named bottleneck and customer chain
- follow-up after earnings or new data
- willingness to discuss risks and falsifiers

Lower conviction is implied by:

- broad basket mentions
- jokes, memes, or one-line replies
- post-hoc recap after large moves
- no mechanism beyond price action

## Risk Map

Track these risks explicitly:

- real position size is unknown
- post may follow a prior price move
- low-liquidity small caps can move because of the account itself
- technical story may be true but already priced
- supplier relationship may be indirect or too small to matter
- revenue timing may lag narrative timing
- claims may be unverifiable or based on incomplete public data
